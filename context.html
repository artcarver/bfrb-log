Neurobiology of BFRBs

Body-Focused Repetitive Behaviors (BFRBs) operate through the cortico-striato-thalamo-cortical (CSTC) circuit. The behavior is maintained by a dopaminergic reinforcement loop in the basal ganglia. When executive function in the prefrontal cortex is depleted by stress or fatigue, the habit circuitry dominates, resulting in automatic or urge-driven motor behaviors.

The Comprehensive Behavioral (ComB) Log

The tracker maps the entire behavioral chain. Capturing these specific variables informs targeted interventions.

Component Clinical Target Mechanism of Action
Pre-Action Motor chain interception Identifies preconscious scanning or postural setups. Allows intervention before the primary tissue damage sequence initiates.
Primary Trigger Arousal state mapping Differentiates hyper-arousal (anxiety) from hypo-arousal (boredom) and executive depletion (fatigue), dictating the necessary counter-measure.
Duration Severity scaling Tracks temporal severity. A 30-minute dissociative episode requires different clinical management than a 30-second transient pick.
Post-Feeling Reinforcement loop Determines if the behavior is maintained by negative reinforcement (relief) or if it generates secondary distress (shame) requiring cognitive defusion.

Intervention Rationale

Tools are categorized by functional state. Acute interventions target physiological overrides, while cold-state tools alter the environmental discriminative stimuli.

Intervention Protocol Mechanism of Action
Motor Block (CR) Habit Reversal Training Relies on motoric incompatibility. By occupying the specific flexor/extensor pathways required for the BFRB, the behavior is mechanically prevented.
Urge Surfing ACT / Extinction Based on Exposure and Response Prevention (ERP). Exposes the nervous system to autonomic arousal without executing the motor response, facilitating habituation and decoupling the urge from the action.
Grounding Vagal Regulation Extended exhalations (4-7-8 ratio) stimulate the vagus nerve, directly increasing parasympathetic tone and downregulating acute amygdala activation.
Stimulus Control Environmental Modification Removes discriminative stimuli (visual cues like mirrors, tactile cues via barriers) to prevent the behavioral chain from activating when executive function is low.
